# The 12D Matrix

MBP uses 12 "dimensions" to map a person's inner structure. These dimensions are not scores used to judge you; rather, they are lenses used to see how different parts of you interact (and where they might conflict). 

They are categorized into 4 main domains:

### 1. Cognitive (How you think)
*   **Abstraction**: Do you think in concrete, practical facts or in abstract, philosophical concepts?
*   **Causal Depth**: Do you only look at surface-level causes of problems, or do you dig deep into systemic roots?
*   **Rigidity/Flexibility**: Is your thinking binary (black-and-white) or fluid and adaptable?

### 2. Emotional (How you feel)
*   **Granularity**: Can you pinpoint exact emotional nuances, or do you just have fuzzy, general feelings?
*   **Regulation**: When you feel intense emotion, is your automatic response to suppress it or express it?
*   **Vulnerability**: How open, raw, and authentic can you actually be with others?

### 3. Relational (How you connect)
*   **Authority Response**: When faced with a leader, do you naturally rebel against them or submit to them?
*   **Recognition**: How strongly do you need external validation and praise from others?
*   **Control**: Do you feel you control your life (internal locus), or does life happen to you (external locus)?

### 4. Adaptive (How you survive)
*   **Stress Response**: When suddenly stressed, does your body fight, flee, or freeze?
*   **Recovery**: How fast do you bounce back from failure or rejection?
*   **Growth**: How efficiently can you turn your old survival patterns into active, conscious strengths?
